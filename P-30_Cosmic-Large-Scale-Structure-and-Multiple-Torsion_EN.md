## 2. Torsion Correction to Structure Formation

### 2.1 Fractal Generalization of Linear Perturbation Theory

In standard cosmology, the evolution of density perturbations $\delta(\vec{x},t)$ is described by the following equation:

$$\ddot{\delta} + 2H\dot{\delta} - 4\pi G \bar{\rho} \delta = 0$$

$$\nabla^{d_s} \Phi = 4\pi G_{eff} \bar{\rho} a^2 \delta$$

Where $d_s$ is the spectral dimension, $G_{eff} = G \cdot (k/k_0)^{d_s-2}$ is the effective gravitational constant.

**Proof**:

1. In fractal spacetime, the Laplacian operator generalizes to fractional order:
   $$\nabla^2 \to \nabla^{d_s} = (-\nabla^2)^{d_s/2}$$

2. Fourier modes of gravitational potential satisfy:
   $$k^{d_s} \Phi_k = 4\pi G \bar{\rho} a^2 \delta_k$$

3. Therefore the effective gravitational constant is:
   $$G_{eff}(k) = G \cdot \left(\frac{k}{k_0}\right)^{d_s-2}$$ ∎

**Derivation**:

   $$d_f^{filament} = 1 + \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{\ln(1+\sigma_8)}{\ln(1+z_{nl})}$$ ∎

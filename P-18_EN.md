## 2 Basic Mathematical Framework

### 2.1 Twist Direction Evolution Equation

The evolution of twist direction is described by **parallel transport of spinor fields**:
$$\frac{d}{dt} |\phi_i(t)\rangle = -i \Omega(t) |\phi_i(t)\rangle$$

Where $\Omega(t)$ is the **connection 1-form** of the fiber bundle, satisfying:
$$\Omega(t) = \tau_{\mu}(t) \gamma^\mu$$

$\tau_{\mu}(t)$ is the twist vector, and $\gamma^\mu$ are Clifford algebra generators.

### 2.2 Definition of Spectral Dimension

Spectral dimension is defined by **heat kernel trace**:
$$d_s(t) = -2 \lim_{\epsilon \to 0^+} \frac{\ln \text{Tr}(e^{-\epsilon D(t)^2})}{\ln \epsilon}$$

Where $D(t)$ is the **time-dependent Dirac operator**, and $\epsilon$ is the diffusion time.

### 2.3 Construction of Time-Dependent Dirac Operator

The time-dependent Dirac operator is modified by the twist tensor $\tau_{\mu\nu}(t)$:
$$D(t) = \gamma^\mu \nabla_\mu + i \gamma^\mu \gamma^\nu \tau_{\mu\nu}(t)$$

Where $\nabla_\mu$ is the covariant derivative, and $\tau_{\mu\nu}(t)$ is the twist tensor, satisfying:
$$\tau_{\mu\nu}(t) = \partial_\mu \tau_\nu(t) - \partial_\nu \tau_\mu(t) + [\tau_\mu(t), \tau_\nu(t)]$$

## 3 Influence of Twist Direction Evolution on Dirac Operator

### 3.1 Time Evolution of Twist Tensor

From the twist direction evolution equation, the time evolution of twist vector $\tau_\mu(t)$ satisfies:
$$\frac{d}{dt} \tau_\mu(t) = -\alpha \nabla^\nu F_{\nu\mu}(t) - \beta \tau^\nu(t) \tau_{\nu\mu}(t)$$

Where $F_{\nu\mu}(t) = \partial_\nu \tau_\mu(t) - \partial_\mu \tau_\nu(t)$ is the twist curvature, and $\alpha, \beta$ are coupling constants.

### 3.2 Time Evolution of Dirac Operator

Squaring $D(t)$:
$$D(t)^2 = -\nabla^2 + i \gamma^\mu \gamma^\nu \nabla_\mu \tau_{\nu}(t) - \tau_{\mu\nu}(t) \tau^{\mu\nu}(t) + \cdots$$

Its time evolution:
$$\frac{d}{dt} D(t)^2 = i \gamma^\mu \gamma^\nu \nabla_\mu \frac{d\tau_\nu(t)}{dt} - 2 \tau_{\mu\nu}(t) \frac{d\tau^{\mu\nu}(t)}{dt}$$

## 4 Heat Kernel Trace Time Evolution

### 4.1 Heat Kernel Expansion

The heat kernel $e^{-\epsilon D(t)^2}$ can be expanded as:
$$e^{-\epsilon D(t)^2} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \epsilon^n a_n(D(t)^2)$$

Where $a_n(D(t)^2)$ are the **Seeley-DeWitt coefficients**.

### 4.2 Trace Time Evolution

Heat kernel trace time evolution:
$$\frac{d}{dt} \text{Tr}(e^{-\epsilon D(t)^2}) = -\epsilon \text{Tr}\left(e^{-\epsilon D(t)^2} \frac{d}{dt} D(t)^2\right)$$

Substituting the expression for $\frac{d}{dt} D(t)^2$:
$$\frac{d}{dt} \text{Tr}(e^{-\epsilon D(t)^2}) = -\epsilon \text{Tr}\left[e^{-\epsilon D(t)^2} \left(i \gamma^\mu \gamma^\nu \nabla_\mu \frac{d\tau_\nu}{dt} - 2 \tau_{\mu\nu} \frac{d\tau^{\mu\nu}}{dt}\right)\right]$$

## 5 Quantitative Derivation of Spectral Dimension Change

### 5.1 Scaling Behavior of Twist Direction Evolution

In the evolution process from parallel to orthogonal twist, the scaling behavior of twist vector satisfies:
$$\tau_\mu(t) \sim t^k$$

Where $k$ is the scaling index, determined by coupling constants $\alpha, \beta$.

### 5.2 Scaling Relationship of Heat Kernel Trace

For parallel twist state ($t \to 0$), system spectral dimension $d_s \to 2$, heat kernel trace satisfies:
$$\text{Tr}(e^{-\epsilon D(0)^2}) \sim \epsilon^{-d_s(0)/2} = \epsilon^{-1}$$

For orthogonal twist state ($t \to t_c$), system spectral dimension $d_s \to 4$, heat kernel trace satisfies:
$$\text{Tr}(e^{-\epsilon D(t_c)^2}) \sim \epsilon^{-d_s(t_c)/2} = \epsilon^{-2}$$

### 5.3 Expression for Time-Dependent Heat Kernel Trace

Considering twist direction evolution, the time-dependent form of heat kernel trace is:
$$\text{Tr}(e^{-\epsilon D(t)^2}) = \epsilon^{-1} f(\epsilon t^m)$$

Where $m$ is the evolution index, and $f(x)$ is the scaling function.

### 5.4 Time Dependence of Spectral Dimension

Substituting the time-dependent heat kernel trace into the spectral dimension definition:
$$d_s(t) = -2 \lim_{\epsilon \to 0^+} \frac{\ln [\epsilon^{-1} f(\epsilon t^m)]}{\ln \epsilon} = 2 - 2 \lim_{\epsilon \to 0^+} \frac{\ln f(\epsilon t^m)}{\ln \epsilon}$$

When $\epsilon \to 0^+$, $\epsilon t^m \to 0$, assuming $f(x) \sim x^n$ ($x \to 0$), then:
$$d_s(t) = 2 - 2n$$

### 5.5 Relationship Between Evolution Index and Scaling Index

From the twist direction evolution equation, the index $n$ of the scaling function and the scaling index $k$ of the twist vector satisfy:
$$n = \frac{2k}{1 + 2k}$$

### 5.6 Quantitative Expression for Spectral Dimension Change Value

Combining the time evolution of the twist tensor, we finally obtain the **change value of spectral dimension with time**:
$$\boxed{d_s(t) = 2 + 2 \cdot \frac{\alpha t}{1 + \beta t^2}}$$

Where:
- $t$ is the coupling time
- $\alpha$ is the topological coupling strength
- $\beta$ is the nonlinear correction coefficient

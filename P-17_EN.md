## 2 Geometric Mechanism of Fractal Helical Torsion Differences

### 2.1 Mathematical Definition of Fractal Helical Torsion

Fractal helical torsion is a local field describing high-speed twist of spacetime fibers, with mathematical expression:
$$\omega^f(x,t) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \omega_n^f(x) e^{i\omega_n t}$$

**Key Components:**
- **n-th order fractal helical density** $\omega_n^f(x)$: Describes the spatial distribution characteristics of the $n$-th twist mode
- **Twist frequency** $\omega_n$: Satisfies quantization condition $\omega_n = \frac{n\hbar}{I}$
- **Time evolution factor** $e^{i\omega_n t}$: Describes the dynamic characteristics of twist

### 2.2 Geometric Representation of Torsion Differences

**Definition 2.1** (Torsion Difference): Let $\omega_i^f(x)$ and $\omega_j^f(x)$ be the fractal helical densities of two different twist modes. Define the torsion difference as:
$$\Delta \omega^f(x) = \omega_i^f(x) - \omega_j^f(x)$$

**Geometric Meaning:**
- $\Delta \omega^f(x) = 0$: Spatial distributions of two twist modes are identical
- $\Delta \omega^f(x) \neq 0$: Spatial distributions of two twist modes are different

### 2.3 Torsion Differences Lead to Twist Vector Differentiation

**Theorem 2.1** (Torsion Differences Lead to Twist Vector Differentiation): Let $\tau_i^\mu(t)$ and $\tau_j^\mu(t)$ be the twist vectors of two twist modes. Then torsion difference $\Delta \omega^f \neq 0$ leads to:
$$\tau_i^\mu(t) \neq \tau_j^\mu(t)$$

**Proof:**

The twist vector is defined as the integral of fractal helical torsion:
$$\tau^\mu(t) = \int_M \omega^f(x,t) \nabla^\mu \omega^f(x,t) d\mu$$

For two different twist modes:
$$\tau_i^\mu(t) - \tau_j^\mu(t) = \int_M (\omega_i^f(x) - \omega_j^f(x)) \nabla^\mu \omega^f(x,t) d\mu$$

If $\omega_i^f(x) \neq \omega_j^f(x)$, then:
$$\tau_i^\mu(t) - \tau_j^\mu(t) \neq 0$$

**Physical Meaning:**
- Torsion differences lead to twist vector differentiation
- Different twist modes have different parallel transport directions
- Spinor evolution paths diverge

### 2.4 Mathematical Derivation: Torsion Differences → Twist Direction Changes

Starting from the twist direction evolution equation:
$$\frac{d}{dt} |\phi_i(t)\rangle = -i \Omega_i(t) |\phi_i(t)\rangle$$

Where $\Omega_i(t) = \tau_i^\mu(t) \gamma^\mu$, and $\tau_i^\mu(t)$ depends on fractal helical torsion:
$$\tau_i^\mu(t) = C \int_M \omega_i^f(x) \nabla^\mu \omega_i^f(x) d\mu$$

When fractal helical densities of different twist modes are different, $\tau_i^\mu(t) \neq \tau_j^\mu(t)$, leading to asynchronous spinor evolution:
$$|\phi_i(t)\rangle = e^{-i \int_0^t \Omega_i(t') dt'} |\phi_i(0)\rangle$$
$$|\phi_j(t)\rangle = e^{-i \int_0^t \Omega_j(t') dt'} |\phi_j(0)\rangle$$

Spinor inner product evolves with time:
$$\langle \phi_i(t) | \phi_j(t) \rangle = \langle \phi_i(0) | e^{i \int_0^t (\Omega_i(t') - \Omega_j(t')) dt'} | \phi_j(0)\rangle$$

When $\Omega_i(t) \neq \Omega_j(t)$, the phase difference of the exponential term accumulates over time, leading to inner product decay:
$$\langle \phi_i(t) | \phi_j(t) \rangle \sim e^{-|\Omega_i - \Omega_j| t} \to 0 \quad (t \to \infty)$$

$$\sum_{n \in G_m} \omega_n^f \sim \sum_{n \in G_n} \omega_n^f$$

**Physical Meaning:**

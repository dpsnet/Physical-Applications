### 2.4 Definition and Properties of Chern Class

**Definition 2.4** (Curvature Form): Let $\Omega$ be the connection 1-form on the principal bundle, then the curvature 2-form is:
$$F = d\Omega + \Omega \wedge \Omega$$

**Definition 2.5** (Chern Class): Let $F$ be the curvature form on the principal bundle, then the $n$-th Chern class is:
$$c_n(F) = \left[\frac{i}{2\pi} \text{Tr}(F^n)\right]$$

where $[\cdot]$ denotes the cohomology class.

**Theorem 2.4** (Topological Invariance of Chern Class): Let $F$ be the curvature form on the principal bundle, then the Chern class $c_n(F)$ is a topological invariant.

**Proof**:

Consider two different connections $\Omega$ and $\Omega'$, with curvatures $F$ and $F'$. Define:
$$\Omega_t = \Omega + t(\Omega' - \Omega), \quad t \in [0,1]$$

Corresponding curvature:
$$F_t = d\Omega_t + \Omega_t \wedge \Omega_t$$

Calculate the difference of Chern classes:
$$c_n(F') - c_n(F) = \frac{i}{2\pi} \int_0^1 dt \frac{d}{dt} \text{Tr}(F_t^n)$$

Using Bianchi identity $DF_t = 0$:
$$\frac{d}{dt} \text{Tr}(F_t^n) = n \text{Tr}(F_t^{n-1} \frac{dF_t}{dt})$$

Since $\frac{dF_t}{dt} = D_t(\frac{d\Omega_t}{dt})$, where $D_t$ is the covariant exterior derivative:
$$\text{Tr}(F_t^{n-1} D_t(\frac{d\Omega_t}{dt})) = D_t \text{Tr}(F_t^{n-1} \frac{d\Omega_t}{dt})$$

Therefore:
$$c_n(F') - c_n(F) = \frac{i}{2\pi} \int_0^1 dt D_t \text{Tr}(F_t^{n-1} \frac{d\Omega_t}{dt}) = 0$$

**Conclusion**: The Chern class is a topological invariant, independent of the choice of connection.

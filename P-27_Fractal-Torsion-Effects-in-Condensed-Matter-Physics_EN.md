## 2. Electron Structure on Fractal Lattices

### 2.1 Band Structure on Sierpinski Carpet

Sierpinski carpet is a classical fractal structure with fractal dimension $d_f = \ln 8 / \ln 3 \approx 1.893$. Consider the tight-binding model:

$$H = -t \sum_{\langle i,j \rangle} (c_i^\dagger c_j + h.c.)$$

Where the sum is only over nearest-neighbor lattice sites on the Sierpinski carpet.

**Theorem 2.1.1 (Fractal Band Theorem)** The electron energy bands on Sierpinski carpet exhibit fractal energy spectra, with energy level distributions satisfying power-law relations:

$$N(E) \sim E^{d_f/2}$$

Where $N(E)$ is the density of states, $d_f$ is the fractal dimension.

**Proof**:

1. From the Schrödinger equation in fractal space:
   $$-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \nabla^2 \psi(x) + V(x)\psi(x) = E\psi(x)$$
   Where $V(x)$ is the fractal lattice potential.

2. Using the scaling transformation properties of fractal measure, eigenvalues satisfy:
   $$E_n \sim n^{2/d_f}$$

3. Density of states is:
   $$N(E) = \sum_n \delta(E - E_n) \sim E^{d_f/2 - 1}$$

4. Therefore cumulative density of states:
   $$N(E) = \int_0^E N(E') dE' \sim E^{d_f/2}$$ ∎

**Corollary 2.1.1 (Fractal van Hove Singularity)** The density of states of fractal lattices exhibits power-law singularities at band edges:

$$N(E) \sim |E - E_c|^{d_f/2 - 1}$$

Rather than van Hove singularities of integer dimensions.

### 2.2 Relationship Between Fractal Dimension and Conductivity

**Theorem 2.2.1 (Fractal Conductivity Scaling Law)** On fractal lattices, the DC conductivity scales with system size $L$ as:

$$\sigma(L) \sim L^{2 - d_s}$$

Where $d_s$ is the spectral dimension, related to fractal dimension $d_f$ by $d_s = 2d_f/(2+\theta)$, $\theta$ being the spectral dimension exponent.

**Proof**:

1. From Einstein relation:
   $$\sigma = e^2 N(E_F) D$$
   Where $D$ is the diffusion coefficient.

2. On fractal structures, diffusion is anomalous:
   $$\langle r^2(t) \rangle \sim t^{2/d_w}$$

3. Diffusion coefficient is scale-dependent:
   $$D(L) \sim L^{2-d_w}$$

4. Spectral dimension is defined as:
   $$d_s = 2d_f/d_w$$

5. Therefore:
   $$\sigma(L) \sim L^{d_f} \cdot L^{2-d_w} = L^{2-d_s}$$ ∎

For Sierpinski carpet, theoretical prediction:

$$\sigma(L) \sim L^{0.20}$$

Indicating that conductivity slowly increases with system size, a characteristic effect of fractal geometry.

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Core Problems in Neutrino Physics

Neutrinos are among the most mysterious particles in particle physics. Since Pauli's prediction of their existence in 1930, neutrino research has achieved revolutionary progress, but several fundamental problems remain:

**Mass Problem**: Neutrinos in the Standard Model are massless, but the solar neutrino problem (Davis experiment, 1968) and atmospheric neutrino anomaly (Super-Kamiokande experiment, 1998) established neutrino oscillations, proving that neutrinos have non-zero masses. Current experiments give mass-squared differences of:

$$\Delta m_{21}^2 = (7.53 \pm 0.18) \times 10^{-5} \, \text{eV}^2$$
$$\Delta m_{31}^2 = (2.51 \pm 0.03) \times 10^{-3} \, \text{eV}^2 \, \text{(NO)}$$

**Absolute Mass**: Cosmological observations give an upper limit on the sum of neutrino masses $\sum m_\nu < 0.12$ eV, while tritium decay experiments give $m_\nu < 0.8$ eV.

**Mixing Problem**: The PMNS matrix describes the transformation between neutrino flavor states and mass states, with parameter values:
$$\theta_{12} \approx 33.5°, \quad \theta_{23} \approx 42°, \quad \theta_{13} \approx 8.5°$$

The origin of these values has no satisfactory explanation to date.

### 1.2 Dilemma of the Standard Model

The Standard Model assigns masses to fermions through the Higgs mechanism, but neutrinos face special dilemmas:

1. **Dirac Mass**: Requires introducing right-handed neutrinos, with extremely small Yukawa coupling ($y_\nu \sim 10^{-12}$), lacking natural explanation
2. **Majorana Mass**: Violates lepton number conservation, requiring new physics beyond the Standard Model
3. **Seesaw Mechanism**: Introduces heavy Majorana neutrinos, but the mass scale is difficult to determine

### 1.3 Perspective of Multiple Torsion Theory

This theory proposes a new perspective: neutrino masses originate from the **multiple torsion structure** of spacetime. The advantages of this framework are:

- **Geometric Origin**: Mass is no longer a free parameter, but determined by spacetime geometry

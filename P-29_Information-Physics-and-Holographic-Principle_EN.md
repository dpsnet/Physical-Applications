The core problem of the information paradox lies in: the contradiction between unitarity of quantum mechanics and general relativity.

$$k = n - 2d_{bulk}$$

Where $k$ is the number of logical qubits, $n$ is the number of physical qubits, and $d_{bulk}$ is the distance in the bulk.

**Proof**:

1. Consider [[n,k,d]] quantum error-correcting code, where $d$ is the code distance

3. Local perturbations in the bulk correspond to non-local operators on the boundary

5. Therefore, $d_{bulk}$ degrees of freedom in the bulk can be reconstructed from the boundary

Where $t_{Page} = \frac{4GM^3}{3\hbar c^4}$ is the Page time, and fractal correction is embodied in the effective central charge $c_{eff} = c \cdot d_f/2$.

**Theorem 4.3.1 (Black Hole Information Conservation Theorem)** Under the fractal holographic framework, the black hole evaporation process maintains information conservation, with the final state being a pure state.

**Proof**:

   $$|\psi(t)\rangle = U(t)|\psi_{in}\rangle$$
